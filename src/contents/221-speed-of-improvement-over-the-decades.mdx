The average speed of improvement in Africa and Europe lead to the rough assumption that Africa develops at the speed that others did in terms of infrastructure:

-   Electrification
    -   [SSA: 1991--2014: 19--37%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/EG.ELC.ACCS.ZS?locations=ZG)
    -   [Europe, UK: 1865--1880: 24.4--77%](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/primary-enrollment-selected-countries)
-   Education
    -   [SSA:  1971--2014- : 46--68%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SE.PRM.CMPT.ZS?locations=ZG)
-   Sanitation
    -   [SSA: 1990--2015 : 23--29%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SH.STA.ACSN?locations=ZG)
-   Water
    -   [SSA: 1990--2015 : 46--67%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SH.H2O.SAFE.ZS?locations=ZG)

This article discusses the challenges of making electricity available and how to achieve energy access to the world's poorest populations, comparing historical rates: [Electricity for All: What Universal Energy Access Will Take](https://thebreakthrough.org/index.php/voices/roger-pielke-jr/electricity-for-all). See the speed of improvement over the past decades here: [www.gapm.io/edafr2](http://www.gapm.io/edafr2).
